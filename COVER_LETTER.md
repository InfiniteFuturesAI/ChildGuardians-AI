# CHILD GUARDIANS — Cover Letter for Policymakers

---

**Re: Open Specification for Child Protection Evidence Infrastructure**

---

## The Problem

Every day, images of child sexual abuse are redistributed across the internet. Each redistribution is a new violation of the same child.

Law enforcement agencies worldwide work to stop this. But they face structural obstacles:

- Evidence collected in one country cannot always be used in another
- Cases collapse in court due to procedural errors made months earlier
- High-profile investigations stall when politically inconvenient
- Officers burn out from repeated exposure to traumatic material
- Victims are re-identified, re-located, and re-victimized — sometimes years after their initial abuse

These are not failures of will. They are failures of infrastructure.

---

## What This Specification Provides

CHILD GUARDIANS is a complete, open specification for evidence management infrastructure. It is designed to:

1. **Preserve evidence in ways that survive court challenge** — chain of custody, authentication, and disclosure requirements are built into the system, not added afterward

2. **Prevent procedural errors before they occur** — the system blocks trial-killing mistakes rather than warning about them

3. **Respect jurisdictional boundaries** — no country can act outside its legal authority; international cooperation follows treaty requirements

4. **Make suppression of inconvenient cases structurally difficult** — oversight is built into the architecture, not dependent on good intentions

5. **Protect officers from unnecessary trauma** — AI pre-filters content before human review

---

## What This Specification Does NOT Do

- ❌ It does not conduct surveillance
- ❌ It does not identify suspects
- ❌ It does not store illegal content
- ❌ It does not bypass warrants or legal process
- ❌ It does not create enforcement power
- ❌ It does not expand beyond child sexual abuse material

The system preserves evidence of material. It never identifies people.

---

## Why This Is Being Shared

This specification is being offered as a public-interest contribution.

- No licensing fees
- No proprietary components
- No vendor lock-in
- No control retained by the author
- No ongoing obligations

The design is open so that any jurisdiction can review, adapt, and deploy it according to their own legal framework — without permission, without payment, and without dependence.

---

## What We Are Asking

We are not asking for adoption, funding, or endorsement.

We are asking for **review**.

Specifically:

- Does this design respect your legal framework?
- Does it address gaps your agencies currently face?
- Are there failure modes we have not anticipated?

The specifications are available for technical, legal, and policy review. We welcome critique. The goal is infrastructure that protects children and survives scrutiny — not infrastructure that avoids it.
